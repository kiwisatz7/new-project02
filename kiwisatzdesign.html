The craftsman who creates a mount has a philosophy.

Philosophy seeks truth.

Truth must be logical and useful.

And it must be real.

Real value aligns with truth.

Design creates symbols.

Users experience symbols and imprint them as images.

Symbols and images are not real, but derivative realities.

The distance between reality, symbol, and image creates tension.

An image that has been confirmed and the tension has disappeared is not the truth.

Tension creates difference.

Difference is value.

We aim for the optimal distance that maintains value.

Between people and products.

Between products and products.

Between humans.
